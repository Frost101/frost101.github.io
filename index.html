Research & ML Projects

  1. SequentialBreak: Large Language Models Can be Fooled by Embedding Jailbreak Prompts into Sequential Prompt Chains

    Privacy and Security of Large Language Models
    Md. Rafi Ur Rashid(Collaborator) - Pennsylvania State University, PA, USA
    Keywords: Large Language Models , Privacy and Security , Adversarial ML
    ResearchGate Link
    ArXiv Link

    This research explores vulnerabilities in large language models (LLMs) by embedding adversarial jailbreak prompts within benign sequential prompt chains. Specifically, we demonstrate how harmful prompts, concealed within otherwise benign inputs, can bypass safety mechanisms and prompt LLMs to generate harmful responses. To address this issue, we introduce SequentialBreak, a novel attack technique that exploits the sequential processing nature of LLMs. We evaluate the effectiveness of SequentialBreak across leading LLMs, including Llama2, Llama3, Gemma2, Vicuna, GPT-3.5 Turbo, and GPT-4. Our findings underscore the critical need for more robust and resilient safeguards to enhance LLM security and mitigate potential misuse.

    Md. Rafi Ur Rashid(Collaborator) - Pennsylvania State University, PA, USA , Vishnu Dasu(Collaborator) - Pennsylvania State University, PA, USA
    Keywords: Multimodal and Vision LLMs , Privacy and Security

    This ongoing collaborative research that surveys privacy and security vulnerabilities in multimodal and vision large language models (LLMs) and explores defense mechanisms.
