About me

Assalamu Alaikum,

Hi, I’m MD Sadik Hossain Shanto, a Computer Science and Engineering researcher and AI engineer. I earned my B.Sc. in CSE from Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET) and am now pursuing my M.Sc. in CSE there, holding a perfect 4.00 CGPA.

My research sits at the intersection of trustworthy and applied AI. I work on the privacy and security of large language and vision-language models, uncertainty in VLMs, and clinical-grade healthcare AI, with seven papers across venues such as ACL, EACL, and Interspeech, alongside ongoing work under review at ACM CCS and Expert Systems with Applications.

I am equally an engineer who ships. As a Remote AI Research Engineer for the Ministry of Defense Healthcare Services in Saudi Arabia, I research, build, and deploy real systems. I architected Digital Harf, a multimodal AI speech and language therapy platform, and Harf-Speech for Arabic phoneme-level assessment, now integrated into Sehhatek, the national healthcare app serving millions of users.

Teaching and mentorship matter deeply to me. Over three years I taught programming and core CS to more than 7,000 students at ROOTs Edu, and I love turning hard ideas into something a beginner can hold onto. I am a strong believer in teamwork and leadership, and I have led teams to national and international recognition in research and cybersecurity.

I believe in pushing limits and embracing every challenge as an opportunity to grow. My life motto is simple: 'Be better than yesterday.' Every day, I aim to learn something new, whether it's a technical skill or a life lesson. I’m driven by the belief that consistent effort, punctuality, and a structured routine are essential to success.

Research & Publications

  1. 1
    Published ACL 2025 (Student Research Workshop) 22 Citations

    SequentialBreak: Large Language Models Can be Fooled by Embedding Jailbreak Prompts into Sequential Prompt Chains

    with Md. Rafi Ur Rashid, PhD Student, Pennsylvania State University, USA

    As large language models move into real products, their safety guardrails stay surprisingly brittle. SequentialBreak exposes a striking blind spot: burying a harmful request inside a chain of ordinary ones slips it past these defenses in a single query, revealing how fragile the alignment really is.

    AI Safety & SecurityLarge Language Models

    with Dr. Mohammad Saifur Rahman, Professor, BUET, Md. Rizwan Parvez, Research Scientist, QCRI

    Today's vision-language models stay confident even when they are wrong. This work tackles a neglected question, do they know what they don't know, delivering the first broad benchmark of their uncertainty and a way to measure it even for closed, commercial models.

    Robustness of Vision Language Models Against Split-Image Harmful Input Attacks

    with Dr. Shagufta Mehnaz, Assistant Professor, Pennsylvania State University, USA, Md. Rafi Ur Rashid, PhD Student, Pennsylvania State University, USA, Vishnu Dasu, PhD Student, Pennsylvania State University, USA

    Vision-language models have learned to refuse harmful images, but only when the harm arrives in one piece.
